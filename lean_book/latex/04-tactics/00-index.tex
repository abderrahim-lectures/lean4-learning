\chapter{Chapter 4: Tactics --- the toolbox for proving things}\label{sec:04-tactics:00-index:1}

Writing proof \emph{terms} directly (as in Chapter 3) quickly becomes hard to manage. Instead, Lean provides \textbf{tactic mode}, entered with \texttt{by}, in which a ``goal'' is worked step by step, much as one would write a proof on paper. This chapter's real subject is not the list of tactics below, but \textbf{how to work a goal whose proof is not yet known}, since that is the skill the rest of the book practices. \textbf{Learning objectives.} By the end of this chapter, read a Lean goal state, use \texttt{intro}/\texttt{exact}/\texttt{apply}/\texttt{rw} to work a goal one step at a time, diagnose a failed tactic from its error message, use \texttt{induction}/\texttt{cases}/ \texttt{constructor}/\texttt{unfold} on inductively-defined data, and carry out a full inductive proof (\texttt{Nat.add}'s commutativity) from scratch.
