\chapter{Chapter 7: Group examples and basic theorems}\label{sec:07-group-theorems:00-index:1}

This chapter proves theorems about an \emph{arbitrary} group. We are given some \texttt{Grp : Group G} for an unknown \texttt{G}, and we use only the fields from Chapter 6. The point of this chapter lies less in the three theorems themselves (they are standard) than in \textbf{the search process} for finding each proof: given a goal, what to examine, what to attempt, and how to recognize being stuck versus one step away. Each theorem below is presented as that search, not merely its answer.

\textbf{Learning objectives.} By the end of this chapter, prove a fact about \emph{every} group at once by reasoning only from \texttt{Group}'s fields, recognize the ``relate both sides to a common third expression'' and ``pad with the identity, then cancel'' patterns, and reuse a proved lemma (\texttt{left\_inverse\_unique}) to shortcut a later one (\texttt{inv\_op}) instead of re-deriving it.
