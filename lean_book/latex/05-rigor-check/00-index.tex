\chapter{Chapter 5: Rigor check --- structures, universes, and equality}\label{sec:05-rigor-check:00-index:1}

Before committing to \texttt{Group}'s definition in the next chapter, this short chapter answers three questions a careful mathematician should already be asking. Why does this book use plain \texttt{structure} instead of Lean's \texttt{class} mechanism, which is what Mathlib actually uses? What exactly is \texttt{Type}, and does \texttt{Group (α : Type)} really quantify over \emph{all} types, including \texttt{Group} itself? And when two proofs both establish \texttt{a = b}, in what sense are they ``the same''? Skipping these would leave exactly the kind of unstated assumption a demanding reader is trained to notice and question, so they are addressed here, before Chapter 6 proceeds.

\textbf{Learning objectives.} By the end of this chapter, explain why this book delays \texttt{class} in favor of \texttt{structure}, state the STLC typing rules and why \texttt{Type} itself needs a universe hierarchy, and distinguish definitional from propositional equality (and predict, correctly, when \texttt{rfl} alone will and will not close a goal).
